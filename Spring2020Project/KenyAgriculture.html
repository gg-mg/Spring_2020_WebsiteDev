Government support is vital for farmers success

The Kenyan government does not have any good policies in place to help her farmers. The government actions seem to hurt the farmers more than help them. I have provided two links below as reported by a leading newspaper in Kenya. In the first article, the government of Kenya ordered importation of 4 million bags of maize. This took in place in early April 2020. By the time i was writing this article, in May 2020, the farmers were trying to use the law courts to seek redress because they had maize stocks which the government had not exhausted before ordering importation. It is worth noting that the minister of agriculture addresses the issue of fertilizer in the first article because the farmers have no access to affordable fertilizer and are at the mercy of middlemen and brokers.

Land Distribution

Land distribution is one a major factor that contributes to poor farm output in Kenya. Majority of the people own less than 5 acres of land which can only be suitable for subsitence farming, not commercial farming. Only a very small percentage of arable land, less than 5%, is under commercial farming use.

Poor farming techniques

Most farmers do not consult agricultural extension officers. This is because the scale of farming does warrant such services. Most farmers depend on rain to grow crops which has become very unpredictable due to global warming.

High level corruption

It is hard to do anything anywhere in the world, especially commercial farming without involving the goverment. Transparency International, an international NGO which monitors corruption ranked Kenya 137/180 in 2019 compared to US which was ranked 12/180 in the same period. Corruption is a hinderance to farming in Kenya
