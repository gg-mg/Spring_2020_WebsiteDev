Maize is the stable food in Kenya and the process of land preparation, planting, weeding, harvesting and drying is time consuming, tedious and wasteful.

Storage of the harvested grain is big challenge to the farmers. The alternatives to traditional granaries are co-operative silos which are often far away requiring farmers to incur transport costs. Farmers can queue for days before they get a chance to offload their produce at the silos. The process also involves hiring of loading and unloading labour plus the cost of renting space in the silo which is unaffordable to most farmers.
